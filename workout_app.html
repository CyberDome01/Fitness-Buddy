Day 1 · Push Day
CHEST & TRICEPS
💪 Mass Builder ~55 min 5 exercises
1
Dumbbell Bench Press Free Weight
Flat Bench + Dumbbells
Sets 4
Reps 8–10
Rest 90 sec
Machine
Exercise
Lie flat on the bench, dumbbells held at chest level, palms facing forward. Press both dumbbells up until your arms are nearly straight, then lower slowly back down to chest level — take 3 seconds on the way down. This is your main mass-builder for chest. The stretch you get with dumbbells is deeper than a barbell, which means more muscle fibers activated. Start lighter than you think — form first, weight second.
2
Chest Press Machine
Selectorized Chest Press
Sets 3
Reps 10–12
Rest 60–75 sec
Machine
Exercise
After the dumbbell press, move to the machine to push more blood into the chest. Sit with your back flat, push handles forward until nearly extended, slow return. The machine stabilizes the weight so you can fully focus on squeezing the chest. After the dumbbell work, this will feel intense — that's exactly the point. Maximize the pump.
3
Pec Deck (Chest Fly)
Pec Deck / Butterfly Machine
Sets 3
Reps 12
Rest 60 sec
Machine
Exercise
Forearms on the pads, bring them together in a hugging motion. At the point where they meet — squeeze hard for 1 second before releasing slowly. This isolation exercise carves detail into the center of the chest. For bulk, it adds fullness across the entire pec. Go lighter here — this is a finishing movement, not a strength test.
4
Cable Tricep Pushdown
Cable Machine — Rope Attachment
Sets 3
Reps 10–12
Rest 60 sec
Machine
Exercise
Stand at the cable station, rope at the top. Hold both ends of the rope, elbows tucked at your sides. Push down and slightly outward at the bottom — flaring the rope at the end maximizes tricep contraction. Slowly bend back to the start. Triceps make up 2/3 of your arm size — this is non-negotiable for arm mass.
5
Overhead Tricep Extension Free Weight
Dumbbell (single, both hands)
Sets 3
Reps 10–12
Rest 60 sec
Machine
Exercise
Sit on a bench, hold one dumbbell overhead with both hands cupped around one end. Lower it behind your head by bending at the elbows, feel the deep stretch in the long head of the tricep, then press back up. The overhead position targets the long head — the biggest part of the tricep and what gives arms that horseshoe thickness from behind.
🔥 Day 1 Bulk TipFor mass, the eccentric (lowering) phase is where muscle damage — and therefore growth — happens most. Count 3 seconds on every downward movement. It feels harder but triggers significantly more hypertrophy than rushing through reps.
Day 2 · Tuesday

Day 2 · Pull Day
BACK & BICEPS
🏋️ V-Taper Builder ~55 min 5 exercises
1
Lat Pulldown
Lat Pulldown Machine
Sets 4
Reps 8–10
Rest 90 sec
Machine
Exercise
Grip slightly wider than shoulders. Pull the bar to your upper chest with your elbows driving down and back. Lean back slightly — about 15 degrees. At the bottom, hold for 1 second and squeeze your lats hard. The lat pulldown is the single most important exercise for building width. Wide back = V-taper. Do your heaviest set first while you're fresh.
2
Dumbbell Bent-Over Row Free Weight
Flat Bench + Dumbbell
Sets 3
Reps 10 each side
Rest 60 sec
Machine
Exercise
Place one knee and one hand on the bench for support. Hold a dumbbell in the opposite hand, arm hanging straight down. Pull the dumbbell up toward your hip — elbow close to your body — then lower slowly. This builds the thickness of the mid-back (rhomboids, lower traps) which adds that 3D muscular look. Don't rotate your torso. Keep hips square.
3
Seated Row Machine
Seated Cable Row
Sets 3
Reps 10–12
Rest 60 sec
Machine
Exercise
Pull to your lower stomach. Squeeze your shoulder blades together hard at the end of each rep — hold 1 second. Slow return. Three back exercises in a row means serious volume on your lats and rhomboids. This volume is exactly what forces muscle growth. By this third exercise your back should be feeling pumped.
4
EZ Bar / Dumbbell Bicep Curl Free Weight
EZ Curl Bar or Dumbbells
Sets 3
Reps 10–12
Rest 60 sec
Machine
Exercise
Stand holding the EZ bar (or dumbbells) with an underhand grip. Curl up to your shoulders, squeeze the bicep at the top, then lower for 3 full seconds. Elbows stay fixed at your sides — don't let them drift forward. This is your primary bicep builder. The slow negative (lowering) is critical for adding mass to the bicep peak.
5
Hammer Curl Free Weight
Dumbbells
Sets 2
Reps 12
Rest 45 sec
Machine
Exercise
Same as a bicep curl but palms face each other (neutral grip) throughout the movement. This hits the brachialis — the muscle under the bicep — which when developed pushes the bicep up and makes your arm look dramatically thicker. Alternate arms or do both simultaneously. Keep it lighter and controlled. Great finisher.
🔥 Day 2 Back TipMost beginners fail to actually engage their back because they pull with their arms instead. Before every set, think "elbows to pockets" — driving your elbows down and back, not just pulling the bar. This one mental cue can double how much you feel your lats working.

Day 4 · Thursday

Day 4 · Lower Body
LEGS — FULL LOWER BODY
🦵 Mass & Power ~55 min 5 exercises
1
Leg Press
Leg Press Machine
Sets 4
Reps 10–12
Rest 90 sec
Machine
Exercise
Feet shoulder-width, mid-platform. Push through your full foot (not just toes) and drive the platform away until your legs are nearly straight. Lower slowly — take 3 seconds down. The leg press is your primary mass builder for quads, glutes, and hamstrings simultaneously. For bulking, gradually add plates week over week. Start with a weight you can handle for 12 clean reps.
2
Leg Extension
Leg Extension Machine
Sets 3
Reps 12
Rest 60 sec
Machine
Exercise
Extend legs to fully straight, hold the peak contraction for 1 full second, then lower for 3 seconds. This quad isolation adds the detail and sweep to the front thigh. For bulking, you want both the compound movement (leg press) AND isolation here to maximize quad hypertrophy.
3
Seated Leg Curl
Seated Leg Curl Machine
Sets 3
Reps 12
Rest 60 sec
Machine
Exercise
Curl heels toward the seat, squeeze hamstrings hard at the bottom for 1 second, then release slowly. Big legs aren't just quads — hamstrings and glutes together make up the back of the leg. Neglecting hamstrings creates imbalance and injury risk. Take the 3-second lowering seriously here.
4
Hip Abductor Machine
Hip Abductor Machine
Sets 2
Reps 15
Rest 45 sec
Machine
Exercise
Pads on the outside of your knees, push legs apart and squeeze the glute med at full range. For men building mass, this stabilizes the hip during heavy compound movements and adds fullness to the outer glute — which makes legs look powerful from behind. Keep resistance light — this is a stability exercise.
5
Calf Raise Machine
Standing Calf Raise
Sets 3
Reps 15
Rest 45 sec
Machine
Exercise
Full range — rise as high as possible, lower until heels drop below the platform for a complete stretch. Calves respond best to full range and a slow tempo. Don't bounce. Hold the peak for 1 second each rep. Calves are notoriously hard to grow — consistency and full range of motion are the only things that move them.
🔥 Day 4 Leg TipLeg day is where most bulk gains happen — your legs are the largest muscle group and training them releases the most growth hormone and testosterone. Push hard here. The discomfort is temporary. The mass is permanent.

SHOULDERS — V-TAPER
🔺 Width Builder ~50 min 5 exercises
1
Shoulder Press Machine
Selectorized Shoulder Press
Sets 4
Reps 8–10
Rest 90 sec
Machine
Exercise
Set the seat so handles are at shoulder height. Press overhead until arms are nearly extended — hold 1 second — then lower slowly for 3 seconds. This is your primary shoulder mass builder. 4 sets here because shoulder width is the cornerstone of the V-taper. Heavier = wider. Push progressive overload on this one every week.
2
Dumbbell Lateral Raise Free Weight
Dumbbells (light)
Sets 3
Reps 12–15
Rest 60 sec
Machine
Exercise
Stand holding light dumbbells at your sides. Raise both arms out to the side to shoulder height — think of pouring water out of a jug (slightly forward tilt). Slow and controlled. Do NOT shrug. Lateral raises build the medial deltoid — the cap of the shoulder — which is the muscle that visually widens your frame the most. Use lighter weight than feels necessary.
3
Lateral Raise Machine
Lateral Raise / Deltoid Machine
Sets 3
Reps 12
Rest 60 sec
Machine
Exercise
After the dumbbell version, the machine allows a strict path to thoroughly fatigue the medial delt. Arms on pads, raise to shoulder height, lower slowly. Double lateral raise (free weight + machine) is an advanced technique for building wide shoulders — don't skip one of them.
4
Cable Face Pull
Cable Machine — Rope Attachment (High)
Sets 3
Reps 15
Rest 45 sec
Machine
Exercise
Set the cable at face height, rope attachment. Step back, pull the rope toward your face while flaring your elbows out wide — like a double bicep pose. Hold for 1 second. This targets the rear deltoid and external rotators. Critical for shoulder health, posture, and the "3D" look of the shoulder from the side. Keep weight light — this is a health exercise as much as a cosmetic one.
5
Rear Delt Machine Optional
Pec Deck (Reverse)
Sets 2
Reps 12
Rest 45 sec
Machine
Exercise
Sit facing the pec deck backward. Grip the handles with arms in front of you, then pull them apart and back in a wide arc. This hammers the rear deltoid — one of the most underdeveloped muscles in beginners. Add this only if you have energy and are under 50 minutes. Skip without guilt if fatigued.
🔥 Day 5 Shoulder TipWide shoulders make your waist look smaller without changing your waist at all — it's pure geometry. Every rep of lateral raises and shoulder press is an investment in that V-taper silhouette. It takes months to see, but it starts today.

Glutes, Legs & Booty Burn
🍑 Glute Focus ~45 min 4 exercises
1
Leg Press Glute Focus
Leg Press Machine
Sets 3
Reps 15
Rest 60 sec
Machine
Exercise
Place feet high and wide on the platform — this is the key to glute activation. Push through your heels, not your toes. Lower slowly to about 90 degrees, then drive back up and squeeze your glutes hard at the top of every rep. Start light — feeling the movement correctly matters more than the weight this week.
2
Hip Abductor Machine Glute Focus
Hip Abductor / Outer Thigh Machine
Sets 3
Reps 15
Rest 45 sec
Machine
Exercise
Pads on the outside of your knees. Push legs apart and pause at maximum range — squeeze the outer glute hard for 1 second before slowly returning. This targets the glute medius, which gives the upper-outer glute its shape and lift. Sit tall throughout. One of the most effective machines for the female physique.
3
Seated Leg Curl
Seated Leg Curl Machine
Sets 3
Reps 12–15
Rest 45 sec
Machine
Exercise
Curl your heels toward the seat, squeezing your hamstrings at the bottom. Hold 1 second, then release slowly. Strong hamstrings balance the leg and make glutes appear rounder and more defined. Don't rush — the slow return is where the muscle work happens.
4
Calf Raise Machine
Standing Calf Raise
Sets 2
Reps 15
Rest 30 sec
Machine
Exercise
Rise onto your toes as high as possible, pause for 1 second, then lower your heels completely below the platform for a full stretch. Full range beats bouncing every time. Toned calves visually elongate your legs — a beautiful finishing touch to lower body day.
✦ Glute Activation TipThe secret to building glutes vs. quads is intent. Feet high and wide on the leg press, push through your heels, and squeeze your glutes at the top. This one cue changes everything. Also: before each set, do a quick mental squeeze of your glutes — this activates the mind-muscle connection that makes the exercise actually work.

Machine
Exercise

✦ Core TipCore training works best with full intention — slow, deliberate reps with a 1-second hold beat fast sloppy reps every time. Your core is also working during every other machine exercise this week, which means it's getting more training than it looks like. Trust the process.
