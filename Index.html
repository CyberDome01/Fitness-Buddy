💪 Mass Builder ~55 min 5 exercises
1
Dumbbell Bench Press Free Weight
Flat Bench + Dumbbells
Sets 4
Reps 8–10
Rest 90 sec
Machine
Exercise
Lie flat on the bench, dumbbells held at chest level, palms facing forward. Press both dumbbells up until your arms are nearly straight, then lower slowly back down to chest level — take 3 seconds on the way down. This is your main mass-builder for chest. The stretch you get with dumbbells is deeper than a barbell, which means more muscle fibers activated. Start lighter than you think — form first, weight second.
2
Chest Press Machine
Selectorized Chest Press
Sets 3
Reps 10–12
Rest 60–75 sec
Machine
Exercise
After the dumbbell press, move to the machine to push more blood into the chest. Sit with your back flat, push handles forward until nearly extended, slow return. The machine stabilizes the weight so you can fully focus on squeezing the chest. After the dumbbell work, this will feel intense — that's exactly the point. Maximize the pump.
3
Pec Deck (Chest Fly)
Pec Deck / Butterfly Machine
Sets 3
Reps 12
Rest 60 sec
Machine
Exercise
Forearms on the pads, bring them together in a hugging motion. At the point where they meet — squeeze hard for 1 second before releasing slowly. This isolation exercise carves detail into the center of the chest. For bulk, it adds fullness across the entire pec. Go lighter here — this is a finishing movement, not a strength test.
4
Cable Tricep Pushdown
Cable Machine — Rope Attachment
Sets 3
Reps 10–12
Rest 60 sec
Machine
Exercise
Stand at the cable station, rope at the top. Hold both ends of the rope, elbows tucked at your sides. Push down and slightly outward at the bottom — flaring the rope at the end maximizes tricep contraction. Slowly bend back to the start. Triceps make up 2/3 of your arm size — this is non-negotiable for arm mass.
5
Overhead Tricep Extension Free Weight
Dumbbell (single, both hands)
Sets 3
Reps 10–12
Rest 60 sec
Machine
Exercise
Sit on a bench, hold one dumbbell overhead with both hands cupped around one end. Lower it behind your head by bending at the elbows, feel the deep stretch in the long head of the tricep, then press back up. The overhead position targets the long head — the biggest part of the tricep and what gives arms that horseshoe thickness from behind.
🔥 Day 1 Bulk TipFor mass, the eccentric (lowering) phase is where muscle damage — and therefore growth — happens most. Count 3 seconds on every downward movement. It feels harder but triggers significantly more hypertrophy than rushing through reps.

🔥 Day 2 Back TipMost beginners fail to actually engage their back because they pull with their arms instead. Before every set, think "elbows to pockets" — driving your elbows down and back, not just pulling the bar. This one mental cue can double how much you feel your lats working.

MEAL TIMING
When You Eat Matters · Optimise Your Window
07:00 – 08:00
Breakfast — Break the Fast
High-protein breakfast within 1–2 hours of waking. Aim for 40–50g protein + complex carbs. Cortisol peaks in the morning — fuelling well prevents muscle breakdown. Examples: eggs + oats + fruit, Greek yogurt + granola, protein shake + banana.
10:30 – 11:00
Mid-Morning Snack
Light protein + carb snack to maintain blood sugar and prevent overeating at lunch. Cottage cheese + rice cakes, handful of nuts + fruit, or a protein bar with minimal sugar.
13:00 – 14:00
Lunch — Largest Meal
Your biggest meal of the day. High protein (50–60g), moderate-to-high carbs, vegetables. Rice + chicken/beef/fish + greens is the classic template. This fuels the afternoon and any evening workout. Avoid heavy fats here if training in the evening.
Pre-Workout (-90 to -30 min)
Pre-Workout Fuel
30–50g fast carbs (banana, white rice, sports drink) + 20–30g protein. Timing depends on session time. For bulking: prioritise carbs here — they fuel intense training and spare muscle protein. Avoid high-fat meals pre-workout as they slow digestion.
Post-Workout (+30 min)
⚡ Post-Workout — The Golden Window
Most important meal for muscle growth. 40–50g fast-digesting protein (whey shake, chicken breast) + 60–80g fast carbs (white rice, banana, potato). The insulin spike from carbs drives amino acids into muscles. Don't skip this window — it's when muscle repair happens most aggressively.
19:00 – 20:00
Dinner
Moderate protein (40–50g), moderate fat, lower carbs. Salmon + sweet potato + salad, or steak + vegetables, or turkey + quinoa. Reduce carbs slightly in the evening compared to daytime meals unless dinner is your post-workout meal.
21:00 – 22:00
Overnight Protein (Optional)
Casein protein shake or cottage cheese (200g). Casein is slow-digesting — it releases amino acids over 6–8 hours while you sleep. This is the exact window when growth hormone spikes and muscle repair is most active. For bulking, this snack can make a significant difference.

SUPPLEMENTS
Evidence-Based Recommendations Only

Only supplements with strong scientific backing. Food first — supplements fill gaps, they don't replace a solid diet.

Creatine Monohydrate
3–5g daily (any time)
The most researched supplement in sports science. Increases muscle phosphocreatine stores, enabling more reps and heavier loads. Gives ~5–10% strength increase within 4 weeks. Also slightly increases muscle water content (good for bulk). Cheap, safe, and highly effective.
✓ Core — Everyone
Whey Protein Powder
1–2 scoops post-workout (25–50g)
Only needed if you can't hit your protein target through food. Fast-digesting, complete amino acid profile. Post-workout timing maximises muscle protein synthesis. Whey isolate if lactose intolerant. Not magic — it's just convenient protein.
✓ Core — if needed
Vitamin D3 + K2
2,000–4,000 IU D3 daily with K2
Most people are deficient, especially in northern climates. D3 supports testosterone production, immune function, bone density, and mood. K2 directs calcium to bones and away from arteries. Take with fat-containing food for best absorption.
✓ Core — Most People
Omega-3 Fish Oil
2–3g EPA+DHA daily
Reduces inflammation from heavy training, supports joint health, and improves recovery. Also has cardiovascular benefits at higher body weights. Look for high-EPA formulas. Take with food to avoid fishy aftertaste. Non-negotiable at heavy training volumes.
✓ Core — Heavy Trainers
Magnesium Glycinate
300–400mg before bed
Improves sleep quality, reduces muscle cramps, and aids recovery. Most gym-goers lose significant magnesium through sweat. Better sleep directly = more growth hormone. Glycinate form absorbs best and doesn't cause digestive issues like oxide form.
Optional · Highly Beneficial
Caffeine (Pre-Workout)
100–200mg, 30–45 min pre-workout
Well-proven to increase strength, endurance, and focus during training. Black coffee works just as well as branded pre-workouts. Cycle it: 5 days on, 2 days off to prevent tolerance build-up. Avoid after 2pm if evening workouts affect your sleep.
Optional · Performance
Zinc
25–30mg daily with food
Critical for testosterone production and immune function — both of which are depleted by heavy training. Highly sweating athletes are commonly zinc deficient. Supports protein synthesis and overall hormone balance. Take away from calcium as they compete for absorption.
Bulk Focus
Casein Protein
30–40g before bed
Slow-digesting protein that feeds muscles over 6–8 hours while you sleep — the primary growth window. Ideal for bulking goals where you want to keep protein synthesis elevated 24/7. Cottage cheese is a cheap, effective food alternative if you don't want a supplement.
